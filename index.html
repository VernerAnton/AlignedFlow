Posture Setup

  • Neutral Neck Position Do a double-chin movement, then release 20–30% of the way back out. Ears over shoulders — this is your target position.
  • Collarbones wide, shoulders heavy Open your chest by widening the ribcage — not by squeezing shoulder blades. Let shoulders drop and feel heavy. Head will naturally retract.
  • Forearms on desk Position forearms roughly half on the desk while typing. This takes load off the neck and shoulders.

Micro Reset

⟳ Reset A — Shoulder Sequence
Shrug shoulders up toward ears → roll them back → drop them down. Repeat 3×. Then take 3 slow belly breaths.
20 seconds
⟳ Reset B — Neck De-bracing
Sit tall. Gently glide your head straight back (subtle — like making a double chin). Do 5 small nods. Hold the last one for 5 seconds.
15 seconds
↗ Levator Scapulae Stretch
Sit tall and drop your right shoulder. Tilt your head to the left and slightly forward at 45°. Feel the stretch from the shoulder-neck junction up toward your skull.

Right side (priority): 20 sec  ·  Left side: 12 sec
~35 seconds total

Long Break

◎ DCF Wall Protocol
Stand or sit with the back of your skull against the wall. Keep your chin level — don't tuck. Gently nod toward your throat (like a subtle double-chin).

Hold 10 seconds → release 5 seconds → repeat 10 reps.

Key: Keep the SCM (front of neck) soft. If it hardens, the movement is too big.
10 reps × 15 sec ≈ 2.5 min
↔ Pec Minor Doorframe Stretch
Stand in a doorframe with your forearms on the frame — elbows at shoulder height, forearms vertical. Step one foot forward and gently lean through the doorway. Feel the stretch across the front of your chest.

1. Elbows at 90° — hold 30 sec
2. Arms in Y-shape (~135°) — hold 30 sec
3. Back to 90° with focus on the sternum — hold 30 sec
90 seconds total
Posture Neck neutral · Collarbones wide · Forearms on desk
